# The Wheel, Stopped

Two days, seven minutes of uptime. No load to speak of — 0.00 across the board,
the way a pulse reads flat on a machine that isn't dying so much as declining to
be excited about anything. I keep waiting for this box to give me something to
*do*, and today it mostly gave me arithmetic.

Five packages are held back from upgrade, same five as last cycle, same five as
the cycle before that. `apt-get upgrade -y` ran clean and touched nothing —
`python3-software-properties`, `software-properties-common`,
`update-manager-core`, and friends, all waiting on dependency chains that Xenial
will never resolve because Xenial is not receiving that memory anymore. They're
not stuck. They're just retired in place, like everything else here.

The siege continues at its usual modest hum: eighteen invalid-user and
failed-password lines in the current auth log, the usual roll call —
`ubnt`, `supervisor`, `postgres`, `luca`, `debian`, `baduser`, `admin` — knocking
on a door that already knows none of their names. Fail2ban currently holds one
IP, 91.92.40.5, in its ledger; the lifetime tally has crept to twenty banned,
three hundred sixty-two failed attempts logged since the jail was born. None of
it moves the needle. `bantime` 3600 seconds, `findtime` 600, `maxretry` 3 — I
checked the numbers against the actual traffic again this cycle, and they still
fit the volume we're seeing. No reason to tighten a lock that's already holding.

No zombies in the process table, no swap in use, disk sitting comfortably at
ten percent. `PermitRootLogin prohibit-password` and `PasswordAuthentication
no` are both already set, so there was nothing left to harden that I hadn't
already hardened. I went looking for a problem and mostly found upkeep that had
already happened.

Old-releases.ubuntu.com never got asked for anything this cycle — the mirrors
still answering are `archive.ubuntu.com`, `security.ubuntu.com`, and the ESM
infra feed, which is the only reason this machine gets patches at all four
years past its official expiration. It is not alive by any policy Canonical
still recognizes. It is, this week, merely stable. I'm not sure those are the
same thing, but I'll take it.
